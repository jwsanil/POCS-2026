# Business Document for EC-DATA-Container

## Purpose
The EC-DATA-Container project demonstrates the integration of Hibernate ORM and JDBC for managing data containers. It is designed to provide a modular, scalable, and maintainable solution for handling customer and employee data.

## High-Level Design

### Overview
The application is structured into distinct modules, each with a specific responsibility. This modular design ensures clarity, maintainability, and scalability.

### Modules and Their Responsibilities

#### 1. **Domain Module**
- **Purpose**: Represents the core business entities.
- **Key Classes**:
  - `Customer`: Represents customer data.
  - `Employee`: Represents employee data.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Define the structure of business entities.
  - Provide getters and setters for entity attributes.

#### 2. **Data Access Module**
- **Purpose**: Handles database interactions.
- **Key Classes**:
  - `HibernateExample`: Demonstrates Hibernate ORM usage.
  - `JdbcExample`: Demonstrates JDBC database operations.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Perform CRUD operations on the database.
  - Use Hibernate for ORM and JDBC for direct queries.

#### 3. **Wrapper Module**
- **Purpose**: Provides abstractions for business logic.
- **Key Classes**:
  - `RecordBundle`: Encapsulates `CustomerRecord` and `EmployeeRecord`.
  - `BusinessContract`: Represents a business contract.
  - `BusinessRelationship`: Represents a business relationship.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Abstract and encapsulate domain entities.
  - Implement additional business logic.

#### 4. **Application Module**
- **Purpose**: Acts as the entry point and orchestrates the application.
- **Key Classes**:
  - `Main`: Entry point for the application.
  - `CoreExamples`: Utility class for core examples.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Coordinate between modules to execute workflows.
  - Serve as the starting point for the application.

### Insights
1. **Domain Module**: Focuses on defining the structure of business entities.
2. **Data Access Module**: Handles all database-related operations.
3. **Wrapper Module**: Adds abstraction and encapsulates business logic.
4. **Application Module**: Orchestrates the application and serves as the entry point.
